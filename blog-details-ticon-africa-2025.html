She Codes, She Leads: The Future of Women in Africa's Tech

From Kampala to Accra, TICON Africa 2025 was electric, full of energy, vision, and collaboration. For Rowena Turinawe, CEO of RukaPay, it was more than a conference; it was a celebration of Africa's boundless potential in technology.

August 22, 2025
RukaPay Editorial Team
8 min read
News

Carrying the inspiration home, she reflected on the incredible connections she made, from familiar faces in Uganda's Marketers Society to innovators reshaping the continent's digital landscape.

A Surprising Dive into ICT

Believe it or not, Rowena didn't plan to dive into ICT. In fact, she had her sights set on a legal career.

"I actually thought I'd become a lawyer," she shares, likely imagining herself in a courtroom, passionately defending the right of the people to... well, maybe not to embrace technology just yet.

Her curiosity about systems, how things work, and the impact of digital tools in finance, education, and daily life led her to a new path.

"ICT found me, and in it, I found my purpose." And who knew that purpose would involve more tech than a lawyer's briefcase?

Starting with roles in project management, network infrastructure, and digital product development, Rowena credits her mentor, Peter Kahiigi, for pushing her beyond her limits. Each step opened new doors, from hands-on tech experience to leadership roles, proving that sometimes, the best plans are the ones you never make.

A Stellar Career Making Waves

Rowena is now the first female CEO of Rhino Payment Services, a top Ugandan fintech. Before this, she led innovation and digital services at Centenary Technology Services and served as Business Transformation Manager at NITA-U, helping craft Uganda's Digital Transformation Roadmap. She's also active with the National BPO and Innovations Council, shaping policies that boost youth-led innovation and local tech scenes.

Recognition & Achievements

She's been recognized as one of Africa's 35 Most Influential Women in Technology (CIO Africa, 2021), co-authored the book Digital Transformation, the NorthStar You Need, and continues to mentor and empower women in ICT, amplifying their voices in leadership spaces. Talk about a multitasker, she's like the Swiss Army knife of tech leadership!

Women in TICON Africa: Leading the Charge

As the first honorary member of TICON Africa's 'Women in Tech' Chapter, Rowena stresses that the platform is more than just about representation, it's about action.

"Women in TICON Africa has immense potential to drive transformational change across the continent by spotlighting and supporting women in technology, innovation, and connectivity."

It's like she's saying, "We're not just here to take up space; we're here to take charge!"

She envisions a space for mentorship, cross-border collaboration, and policy influence, bridging the gender gap in ICT and ensuring women aren't just present, they're thriving and leading. Because let's face it, who wouldn't want a world where women are at the helm of tech innovation?

Tech as a Tool for Inclusivity

Rowena believes tech can break down barriers. From mobile access in rural areas to empowering women entrepreneurs with digital payments, she emphasizes that true inclusion needs intentional design, policies, and user-focused innovation.

"Tech will continue to dismantle traditional barriers, geographic, economic, even linguistic, if we prioritize local context and inclusive solutions. The future is a digital Africa that works for everyone, not just the privileged few."

It's like she's handing out digital keys to the kingdom!

Facing Challenges with Strength

Like many women in ICT, Rowena has dealt with bias and the pressure to prove herself. She credits her faith, mentorship, and unwavering commitment for helping her overcome obstacles.

"I learned to speak up, trust my results, and embrace every opportunity to grow. God's guidance has been my anchor through the challenges."

A true testament that sometimes, the strongest armor is a good dose of faith and a supportive network.

Envisioning Africa's Digital Future

TICON Africa 2025 wasn't just an event, it was a reminder that leadership in tech is about connection, courage, and purpose. Rowena Turinawe's journey shows the power of resilience, mentorship, and inclusion in shaping a digital Africa where women and youth lead, innovate, and thrive.

At RukaPay, we celebrate leaders like Rowena, trailblazers who inspire the continent to embrace a digital future that is accessible, inclusive, and unstoppable. Here's to a future where everyone has a seat at the digital table!

Rowena Turinawe

CEO, RukaPay (Rhino Payment Services)
First female CEO of Rhino Payment Services and one of Africa's 35 Most Influential Women in Technology. A passionate advocate for women in tech and digital transformation across Africa.
